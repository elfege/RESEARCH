## About

This repository contains ongoing academic essays bridging Hegelian dialectics, philosophy of logic, and artificial intelligence theory. The work extends research begun in my doctoral dissertation and continued through teaching, publication, and — more recently — the practice of software engineering.

The central thread: the structures Hegel identified in the *Science of Logic* (1812–1816) are not merely philosophical constructs. They are formal patterns that manifest in arithmetic, in learning algorithms, and in the conditions under which any system — biological or artificial — can be said to grow.

## Current Essays

### 1. L'Aufhebung arithmétique : une proposition
**The Arithmetic Aufhebung: A Proposition**

Three lines of elementary arithmetic expose the structure of the Hegelian Aufhebung:

```
1 + (-1) = 0        — negation as annihilation (understanding)
1 + |-1| = 2        — negation preserved, produces more (Aufhebung)
|-1| = (-1)²        — absolute value = negation of negation (reason)
```

Absolute value is the Aufhebung seen by the understanding; squaring is the Aufhebung seen by reason. Both yield the same result, but one conceals the mediation while the other shows it.

### 2. La contingence comme réservoir de détermination
**Contingency as a Reservoir of Determination: From Arithmetic Aufhebung to a Logic of Growth**

Extends Essay 1 into contingency theory and machine learning. Key theses:

- **Contingency is not randomness.** It is a *saturated zero* — the co-presence of opposed necessities whose resultant is null but whose content is maximal.
- **Two types of friction:** δ₁ (logical, *a priori* — contradiction implied by any position) and δ₂ (empirical — contingency encountered).
- **A dialectical alternative to gradient descent:**

```
W_{n+1} = W_n + α₁δ₁² + α₂δ₂²
```

Where current ML subtracts error (convergence → functional death), a dialectical update adds the square of friction (growth → elevation).

### Doctoral Dissertation

**Une critique hégélienne de Hegel : et une introduction à la lecture de Hegel**
*A Hegelian Critique of Hegel: and an introduction to reading Hegel*

Ph.D. in Philosophy, Université de Nantes, 2014. 450 pages.
Research laboratory: CAPHI (Centre Atlantique de Philosophie), CEI, E 504.

The dissertation identifies an internal insufficiency in Hegel's treatment of the category of **quantity** in the *Science of Logic*. Quantity is structurally central to Hegel's system — it is the hinge between Being, Essence, and Concept, transversal across all three moments, and the category under which all others are gathered at the conclusion. Yet Hegel dismisses it as "merely a determined degree of the logical Idea" and "speculatively unfruitful." The dissertation argues this is incoherent and proposes a corrective reading.
